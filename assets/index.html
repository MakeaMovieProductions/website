How to Use It

Shot List Template

A 15-column, 500-row production sheet. Seven steps from blank template to locked shot list.

XLSX ~35KB Version 2 Google Sheets Compatible Free

Step 01 of 07

02

Number Scenes & Shots

Scene # maps directly to your script's scene number. Shot # is the sequential number within that scene. Both are free text — use "3A", "12", or whatever matches your breakdown.

03

Set INT / EXT & Location

Colour-coded dropdown — INT (interior), EXT (exterior), or INT/EXT (mixed). Also, add the specific location: "Park Bench", "Hospital Corridor Level 2", etc.

01

Fill Production Info

Complete the header block at the top — project name, director, 1st & 2nd A.D., D.P. on the left; shoot date, call time, wrap time, and set location on the right. This block stays frozen as you scroll.

02

Number Scenes & Shots

Scene # (col A) maps directly to your script's scene number. Shot # (col B) is the sequential number within that scene. Both are free text — use "3A", "12", or whatever matches your breakdown.

03

Set INT / EXT & Location

Col E is a colour-coded dropdown — INT (interior), EXT (exterior), or INT/EXT (mixed). Col F is free text for the specific location: "Park Bench", "Hospital Corridor Level 2", etc.

04

Define Your Shot Language

Four dropdown columns define your visual grammar: Shot Size (G), Camera Angle (H), Framing (I), and Camera Movement (J). Every standard cinematic option — EWS to ECU, Eye Level to Dutch Angle — is in the lists.

05

Note Equipment & Lens

Col K is free text — note the camera body, lens, and any rigs or supports for that specific shot. "Sony FX3 + G Master 85mm + RS4 Gimbal" or just "Tripod + 50mm". This column drives your gear checklist.

06

Estimate Time Blocks

Setup Time (col L) = time to physically position camera, lighting, and crew. Shoot Time (col M) = total filming duration. Add both to get the full time block per shot and build a realistic shooting schedule.

07

Track Status & Lock Takes

Shoot Status (col C) is your on-set dashboard: To Shoot → In Progress → Wrapped. After the shoot, enter the chosen take in Final Take # (col N), then set Status (col O) to ✅ Done or ⚠️ Reshoot needed.
